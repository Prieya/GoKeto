I have never done this since started working out and wow it's such a motivational kick! Makes me really proud to see that I've set a goal and managed to stick to it ! even more so mentally than the physically! I have always struggled with procrastination and perseverance so this was going to be my chance to prove to myself that I could beat this self sabotaging loop I often get stuck into. If someone would have told me last year that I'd be super into a fitness challenge and enjoying a 6 weeks of daily workout I would've laughed for days!! I didn't do any sort of physical exercise in the past 16 years, being lazy, weak, smoking.. And here I am at the end of these 42 sweaty days telling myself : YOU DID IT!!

I started working out around March 2020 when lockdown first began and was doing a mix of YouTube workouts and going to the gym when it was open. Then in October 2020 I ditched my gym membership completely and have been doing Youtube workouts pretty much every day since. I used to go to the gym about 4-5 days a week but never really saw any progress. I wasn't gaining weight but I wasn't seeing any difference in my body either. I did gymnastics all throughout my childhood/most of my teenage years and was always very toned as a result. After stopping gymnastics, my stomach became one of my biggest insecurities; all I wanted was my gymnast abs back! Luckily for me, I was wrong! After discovering Youtube workouts I'm slowly getting back to be being as strong and as fit as I've ever been.

When I look at this photo I don’t just see a physical transformation that pleases me, but a mental one too. I am both physically and mentally stronger in the one on the right, my attitude towards food is so much better now and my outlook of what fitness is has changed greatly. I used to measure progress purely on the number I saw on the scale but since joining the Doctor Lean, I’ve started to take note on how I feel as a measure of progress. I do a combination of weightlifting and Youtube videos (mainly weightlifting) 6 days a week and I actually enjoy it?! The me on the left (2020) would never have thought I’d be this committed to myself and have such a positive outlook on fitness and nutrition. I make sure that every day, even on rest days (especially on rest days!) that I give myself enough protein, enough carbs etc that my body needs to not just function, but thrive. And as a result, my mind and soul thrives.

I’d just opened my own practice and was trying to make ends meet while also going through a tough breakup, Then my father passed away from cancer. Through it all, I self-medicated with food, ballooning up to 228 pounds. My dad death was a wake up call, I found a new purpose.I started lifting weights with a “bro split” [body-part training] five to six days a week. I started by eating most of the same foods, but limiting carbs. About six weeks in, I was down 10 pounds.I started doing compound lifts at the beginning of each body part. I also cut back on cardio and revamped my diet to include more lean meats, Greek yogurt, avocados, and carbs from sweet potatoes and whole grains. I was doing cardio six days a week after lifting, which is too much. Now I keep it to three, max. My bench press went up from 185 to 275 for 4–6 reps. I can squat 275 for four. And here I am at my strongest.
